# MAP 22 Visual Extraction

MAP 22 is not the static background of the finished website. It is the visual target state: the image the system gradually approaches as live evidence, news, reports, legal rulings, field observations, natural events, and community material populate the two sides.

## Core spatial rule

The river is the border between the two worlds. The border is not a generic line, scale, or UI divider.

The river functions as:

- visual boundary
- methodological seam
- living threshold
- evidence transition path
- anchor for shared / contested entries

## Two earth figures

MAP 22 contains two figures representing land under two approaches.

### Left earth figure: compressed / extracted / constrained land

Approximate location: left-center to lower-left body mass.

Interpretation:

- earth as wounded body
- carved, mined, cut, and vertically compressed
- visible cavities and strata
- anthropomorphic mass, closer to body / trunk / wound than to neutral terrain
- associated with control, extraction, confinement, hunting pressure, animal coercion, overworked soil, and monoculture logic

Important detail:

- black dog chained to a tree or tree-like structure, located in the left side, middle-lower area
- this must be preserved as a semantic element related to animal welfare, coercion, captivity, and extractive relation to animals

### Right earth figure: relational / vegetated / hydrological land

Approximate location: regenerative half, distributed through canopy, slope, stream, rocks, understory, and living ground.

Interpretation:

- earth as living distributed body
- not one isolated figure but a body-landscape
- vegetation, water, roots, rocks, Paulownia trees in flower, fungal life, and animal presence are continuous
- represents regenerative relation, not decorative greenness
- includes future capacity for integrated solar infrastructure and solar drones monitoring fire, air, and soil

## Data-to-element principle

Each visual element added to the scene must correspond to a data event or curated evidence entry.

Possible data events:

- scientific study
- retracted paper
- legal ruling
- law or policy update
- funding shift
- market or subsidy shift
- field report
- natural event
- fire risk / air / soil monitoring signal
- community gallery upload
- fact-check or claim correction
- court or administrative decision

The scene should not populate randomly. It should accumulate through evidence.

## Initial state vs target state

Initial state:

- river border visible
- two earth figures only partially present
- few seed elements
- limited hotspots
- enough structure to show the two logics without completing the final image

Live state:

- elements appear, thicken, mutate, or are corrected as entries accumulate
- left side becomes more visibly saturated by extractive evidence
- right side expands into a regenerative museum of practices, relations, and monitored living systems

Target state:

- MAP 22 complete or near-complete
- dense accumulation of evidence-derived elements
- visual complexity readable as historical and live data sedimentation

## Extraction categories

Left / extractive:

- compressed earth body
- carved strata
- cavities / tunnels
- chains / restraint
- black dog chained to tree
- monoculture blocks
- dead or controlled trees
- machinery / extraction hints
- hunting / agri-hunting markers
- soil erosion / exposed ground
- structural rupture

Right / regenerative:

- river / stream / water border
- vegetated body-landscape
- canopy and understory
- mushroom / fungal zones
- rocks and living soil
- Paulownia trees in flower, where previously mistaken for purple flowers
- pollinator markers connected to flowering trees and habitat evidence
- wetland / water-retention features
- animal-individual presence
- integrated solar panels
- solar drones for fire, air, and soil monitoring
- community / care / practice objects

## Implementation note

Do not flatten MAP 22 into one background image for production. Use it as a reference target and extract visual elements into a manifest-driven scene system.
